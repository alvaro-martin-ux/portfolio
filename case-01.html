Case Study — Understanding User Segments to Improve Engagement & Retention in a Short-Form Content Editing App

Focus: user research, retention, attrition strategy, insights, personalization and onboarding.

01 — Context

The product is a mobile-based creative platform for short-form video editing tools for short-form creators, where users are given an option to enhance and share user-generated content.

The purpose of this project was to:

  • Identify who the most engaged users are
  • Identify why some users disengage from the experience
  • Develop more personalized onboarding and education recommendations
  • Aid product, marketing, and streaming strategies
i

This case study summarizes the research process without disclosing all of the variables and metrics due to NDA requirements.

02 — My Responsibilities

03 — Methods

A mixed-methods approach was used, combining three complementary frameworks to triangulate findings.

Onboarding Survey (non zero-time summary)

Ran a mix of attitudinal and behavioral questions to:

  • Understand existing skill difficulties at start
  • Map competing products used by users
  • Understand key motivational factors
  • No incentivized (e.g., planned, non-promotional)
Behavioral Analytics
  • Sessions analyzed
  • Retention rates
  • Segmentation of user gestures
  • In-app notification flow types
  • Previous apps survey and other variables
Synthesis & Clustering
  • Affinity mapping for patterns
  • Two persona/segment archetypes
  • Study-provided recommendations
  • Measuring outcomes
  • Emergent opportunities
Tools
  • Amplitude
  • Dovetail
  • Notion
  • Figma / FigJam
  • AI-assisted synthesis

04 — Key Insights (Anonymized)

The data revealed two distinct user segments with very different needs, behaviors, and motivations around the product and its features.

i

In our analysis, these two main user segments differed along several organizational behaviors we found in the data: content, creation, and skill level.

Segment A
Informal Creators
  • Use the app for personal ideas
  • Prefer simple, quickly-working editing tools
  • Get easily overwhelmed by features
  • Are motivated by fun and self-expression
  • Go on short bursts of activity from free time
Segment B
Professionals
  • Use the app for branding, content planning, or client deliverables
  • Prefer more features and customization
  • Value efficiency and production quality
  • More tolerant of friction if outcomes are valuable
  • Willing to pay for tools or subscriptions

These two segments also have a consistent difference in their patterns, problems, and values. This means they need different kinds of patterns, products, and value propositions.

05 — Opportunities Identified

Through segmentation, we identified opportunities to improve experience relevance and engagement on both user types.

For Informal Creators
  • Better onboarding experience
  • Personalized experience for activities
  • Better map of what to look for
  • Applying better UX or social screen components
For Professionals
  • More professional content
  • Better productivity tools
  • Smarter saving of activity and time
  • Personalization of needed templates
  • Pro subscriptions

These recommendations were shared with Product, Design, and Data teams to align on product decisions.

06 — Impact

Through strong results in several conversations, the project had a positive outcome as a baseline to assess:

  • Product alignment with clearer growth objectives for the design
  • The onboarding experience was improved based on segment-awareness improvements
  • Recommendations actively being built into engineering and product decisions
  • Partial rollout of framework for filtering and tailoring targeted recommendations
  • Further rollout in audience segmenting and awareness of sharing
07 — Learnings

This project reinforced some key principles:

01
Segment-first analysis is more powerful than feature-first.
Understanding who users are before what they do leads to sharper insights.
02
Assumptions often mask real patterns — data confirmed them here.
Early hypotheses were partially confirmed, giving the team confidence to move fast.
03
Always align product outcomes to specific user segments to accelerate growth.
